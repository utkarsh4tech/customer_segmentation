## **Project Report: Customer Segmentation for Business Intelligence**

### **1. Executive Summary**

This analysis utilizes the Online Retail II dataset (2009–2011) to segment customers using RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) modeling and the K-Means Clustering algorithm. The goal is to transform raw transactional data into actionable insights, enabling targeted marketing and optimized resource allocation.

### **2. Analysis Process & Methodology**


#### **Phase 1: Data Preparation & Sanity Checks**

**Data Aggregation:** The dataset consisted of over 1 million records across two years. Data was merged and reviewed for inconsistencies.

**Handling Cancellations:** Invoices starting with "C" (representing cancellations) and "A" (bad debt adjustments) were identified. These involve negative quantities and prices, which can skew behavioral analysis.

**Data Cleaning:** Removed rows without Customer ID (crucial for tracking unique behavior).

Filtered out non-product transactions (e.g., "Postage", "Manual") using Regex on StockCode to ensure segments are based purely on shopping habits.

Removed rows with a price of zero or negative quantities to maintain financial integrity.

#### **Phase 2: Feature Engineering (RFM Analysis)**

We calculated three key metrics for each unique customer:

**Recency:** Days since the last purchase (lower is better).

**Frequency:** Total number of unique invoices (higher shows loyalty).

**Monetary Value:** Total revenue generated by the customer.

#### **Phase 3: Outlier Handling & Scaling**

**Outlier Removal:** Extreme spenders (wholesalers) and exceptionally frequent shoppers were separated before clustering to prevent them from pulling the "centroids" away from the average consumer base.

**Standardization:** Used StandardScaler to normalize the data. Since K-Means uses Euclidean distance, scaling ensures that a high monetary value (£10,000) doesn't overshadow recency (10 days).

#### **Phase 4: K-Means Clustering**

The **Elbow Method** and **Silhouette Scores** were evaluated to find the optimal number of clusters (k=4 was selected for the final segmentation).

**Labeling:** Clusters were assigned based on their RFM profile characteristics (e.g., High Frequency vs. High Recency).

Based on the 3D scatter plots and feature distributions, here are the strategic recommendations for the identified segments:

| Name          | Key Features (RFM Behavior)                           | CTA                  | Insight                                                                        |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- | -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **CHAMPIONS** | High Monetary • High Frequency • Low Recency          | Reward & Retain      | Core revenue drivers. Highly engaged and loyal customers.                      |
| **LOYALISTS** | Medium Monetary • Medium Frequency • Moderate Recency | Nurture & Upsell     | Strong potential to become Champions with the right push.                      |
| **AT-RISK**   | Low Monetary • Low Frequency • Medium Recency         | Re-Engage            | Customers are losing interest and may churn soon.                              |
| **CHURNED**   | Low Monetary • Low Frequency • High Recency           | Win-Back             | Inactive customers; recovery is difficult but possible with strong incentives. |
| **ELITES**    | Extremely High Monetary • Extremely High Frequency    | Protect & Prioritize | Top 1% users contributing disproportionate revenue.                            |
| **SPLURGERS** | Very High Monetary • Low/Normal Frequency             | Personalize & Upsell | High spend per purchase but low engagement frequency.                          |
| **REGULARS**  | Very High Frequency • Low/Moderate Monetary           | Bundle & Upsell      | Frequent buyers with low basket size; opportunity to increase AOV.             |
